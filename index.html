Choosing the Right Partner in Auto Transport

Whether you're shipping your car across the state or across the country, one of the first decisions you’ll face is whether to hire a broker or a carrier. Each plays a different but vital role in the auto transport industry. This guide will help you compare both options and decide which suits your needs best—with expert insight from AmeriFreight.

What Do Auto Transport Brokers Do?

A broker is your logistics partner. They connect you with carriers who will physically transport your vehicle. Brokers like AmeriFreight have access to a wide network of licensed, insured carriers and help negotiate the best price and timing for your shipment.

What Do Carriers Offer?

Carriers are the transport companies that own the trucks and do the actual vehicle shipping. Working directly with a carrier can sometimes save you broker fees, but it may also limit your options and control over timing, pricing, or route flexibility.

Comparing Brokers and Carriers

Brokers (e.g., AmeriFreight)

  • Extensive network of carriers
  • Handles all communication and paperwork
  • Better availability and scheduling
  • Can secure competitive pricing
  • Added protection through vetted services

Carriers (Direct Contact)

  • May offer direct pricing
  • Handles the shipment themselves
  • Limited routes and availability
  • Usually no customer service team
  • Requires more research on your part

Cost Considerations

While going direct might seem cheaper, brokers often provide better value. They leverage relationships with carriers to get you lower rates, and they prevent costly mistakes like unvetted carriers or hidden fees.
